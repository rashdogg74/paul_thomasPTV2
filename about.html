My Training philosophy

My idea of training is "We were built to do this, we were designed to move and there are only positive effects from being active". Unfortunately, the rhythm, distraction, obligations, and stress of modern life present lots of obstacles for people being active. My speciality is helping people see the worth and importance in being active and helping them to realise their potential. Unlocking this potenital can affect all aspects of their lives, giving a renewed sense of self worth, contributing to their mental and physical well-being, through practicing resilience, planning and achieving goals and objectives and being social while doing so. I believe that true growth comes from the difficult moments in life and when we start to embrace these tough moments, when we struggle, when we try and try again, we become stronger both physically and mentally from it. Being the best you can be requires a lifetime of practice, commitment to yourself and others and many failures along the way, but it's through this process that many of us see our true identities.

My message

After 20+ years of being active (but not neccessarily always healthy) I feel like I have developed a deep understanding of the key barriers to an active healthy lifestyle as well as some of the major motivators to help people make a change. My goal is to help people realise their potential and to do so I think it's importnat tht people understand that we all start from different places. From 10-16 years old when I was a little chubby kid trying to keep up with the stronger and faster kids in football, basketball, athletics and our national game Gaelic Football, I always wished of being in better shape however, lacked the knowledge, understanding and guidance to do so. The more people I spoke to, the more questions I asked and the more I learned from teachers, role models and eventually lecturers and peers, the fitter I seemd to become. Small changes over time allowed me to achieve my fitness goals, become better at my chosen sports, feel healthier and happier. I then decided to study "Sports and recreation" and the rest as they say is history. Nobody ever starts out living a fantastically healthy lifestyle and stays like that forever, life has many ups and downs and it is a long journey for us all but it's important to remember that you can be in the best shape of your life at any age. All it takes is making a decision and reaching out for help to get started.
